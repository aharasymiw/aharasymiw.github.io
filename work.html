Career Themes

Looking across my professional experience, several consistent themes emerge that define my approach to work:

Building Tools That Empower Others

Whether creating data pipelines, teaching bootcamp students, or developing internal tools, I focus on enabling others to do their work more effectively. The best code I've written isn't impressive for its complexity—it's valuable because it solves real problems for real people.

Leading Through Influence

Much of my impact has come from leading without formal authority. By sharing best practices, improving documentation, and mentoring colleagues, I've influenced teams and organizations to adopt better approaches. This skill translates directly to developer advocacy work.

Learning in Public

I've never been afraid to work with unfamiliar technologies or admit when I'm learning. Whether picking up Rust quickly at Augeo or teaching web development concepts I'd just mastered at Prime, I believe in transparent learning and sharing the journey with others.

Communication as Core Skill

From consulting with retail customers to teaching complete beginners, communication has been central to every role. I've learned to translate between technical and business stakeholders, explain complex concepts clearly, and build relationships across diverse audiences.

Quality Over Speed (Usually)

I believe in writing code that others can understand and maintain. While I can move quickly when needed, I prefer to balance speed with thoughtfulness—comprehensive tests, clear documentation, and architectural decisions that serve long-term needs.
