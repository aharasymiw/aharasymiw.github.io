Why Tabletop RPGs Matter

At their core, tabletop role-playing games like Dungeons & Dragons are about collaborative storytelling, creative problem-solving, and building connections between people. For over a decade, I've used TTRPGs as a vehicle for community building, teaching, and creating spaces where everyone feels welcome at the table.

What started as a personal hobby evolved into a passion for organized play, event coordination, and helping newcomers discover the magic of shared storytelling. The skills I've developed running games—improvisation, facilitation, conflict resolution, and creating inclusive environments—translate directly to my work in software education and developer advocacy.

Dungeon Master Experience

2014 - Present

Since 2014, I've been running tabletop RPG games for diverse audiences, from complete beginners to experienced players. My approach focuses on creating welcoming, inclusive tables where everyone can contribute to the story and have fun, regardless of their experience level.

Game Master Philosophy

  • Player-Centered Storytelling: The best sessions emerge from player choices and character development, not predetermined plots
  • Inclusive Tables: Everyone deserves to feel safe and welcome, with clear expectations and consent-based gameplay
  • Teaching Through Play: New players learn best by jumping in, with supportive guidance along the way
  • Improvisation: The most memorable moments come from unexpected directions, requiring adaptability and quick thinking
  • Shared Authority: Great games emerge when everyone at the table contributes to the collaborative narrative

Game Systems & Experience

While Dungeons & Dragons remains my primary system, I've run and played numerous other TTRPGs:

  • D&D 5th Edition (primary expertise)
  • Pathfinder and Pathfinder 2nd Edition
  • Various indie and story-focused systems
  • One-shot systems for conventions and events

Skills Developed

  • Facilitation: Managing group dynamics and ensuring everyone gets spotlight time
  • Improvisation: Responding to unexpected player choices while maintaining narrative coherence
  • Conflict Resolution: Navigating disagreements and ensuring table harmony
  • Teaching: Introducing complex rule systems to newcomers without overwhelming them
  • Storytelling: Crafting engaging narratives that respond to player agency
  • Emotional Intelligence: Reading the room and adjusting pacing and tone accordingly

Fantasy Flight Game Center Organized Play

2016 - 2018

Founded and organized a public play program at Fantasy Flight Game Center, Minnesota's largest game publisher and retailer. This initiative transformed casual drop-in gaming into a structured, welcoming community that introduced hundreds of people to organized tabletop RPG play.

Program Overview

Created a weekly public play program that welcomed strangers to join organized D&D games at the game center. The program focused on accessibility for newcomers while providing experienced players with consistent, quality gaming experiences.

Key Responsibilities

  • Recruited and trained volunteer dungeon masters
  • Scheduled weekly game sessions and managed table logistics
  • Created onboarding materials for new players and DMs
  • Coordinated with Fantasy Flight staff on space and resource needs
  • Built and maintained an engaged community through online channels
  • Resolved conflicts and ensured inclusive, safe gaming environments

Developing the DM Pipeline

One of the program's greatest successes was creating a pathway from new player to experienced dungeon master:

  • Identified promising players with potential for game mastering
  • Provided mentorship and training on running sessions
  • Created opportunities for new DMs to run shorter adventures
  • Offered ongoing support and feedback to developing DMs
  • Built a community where DMs shared best practices and resources

Community Impact

  • Hundreds of players introduced to organized play
  • Dozens of new dungeon masters trained and mentored
  • Consistent weekly attendance creating stable community
  • Welcoming environment for underrepresented gamers
  • Model program referenced by other stores and communities

Lessons in Community Building

This experience taught me invaluable lessons about growing and sustaining communities—lessons that apply directly to developer relations work:

  • Lower the Barrier to Entry: Make it easy for newcomers to join and contribute
  • Create Leadership Pathways: Help community members grow into leadership roles
  • Set Clear Expectations: Explicit community guidelines prevent problems
  • Celebrate Contributions: Recognition motivates continued engagement
  • Listen and Adapt: The best communities evolve based on member needs

Public Play Campaigns

2016 - Present

Consistently run public play campaigns including Adventurers League (D&D's official organized play program) and other structured gaming experiences. This ongoing commitment has helped hundreds of players experience quality tabletop gaming in welcoming environments.

What is Adventurers League?

Adventurers League is D&D's official organized play program, allowing players to bring characters to different tables and events while following a shared continuity. As an AL DM, I've helped facilitate this portable gaming experience for countless players.

Running Organized Play

  • Hosting regular weekly or monthly sessions
  • Following official organized play guidelines
  • Managing drop-in players and varying table composition
  • Tracking character progression and rewards
  • Creating memorable experiences within structured constraints

Benefits of Organized Play

  • Consistent scheduling builds reliable community
  • Drop-in structure accommodates busy schedules
  • Shared rules framework reduces barriers for new DMs
  • Character portability increases player investment
  • Official support provides quality adventures

The Intersection of Gaming and Professional Skills

Running tabletop RPGs might seem disconnected from software development and developer advocacy, but the skills overlap significantly:

Facilitation & Collaboration

Like running effective meetings or workshops, DMing requires managing group dynamics, ensuring everyone participates, and keeping discussions on track while allowing for organic exploration. I've learned to read the room, adjust on the fly, and create environments where diverse voices contribute.

Teaching Complex Systems

Explaining D&D rules to complete beginners mirrors teaching programming concepts—both require breaking complex systems into understandable chunks, providing scaffolded learning experiences, and knowing when to simplify versus when to introduce nuance.

Improvisation & Problem-Solving

Players constantly surprise DMs with unexpected approaches. Responding improvisationally while maintaining narrative coherence is similar to troubleshooting code issues or adapting technical talks when audience questions take things in new directions.

Community Building

Growing the Fantasy Flight gaming community taught me how to cultivate inclusive spaces, develop leadership pipelines, and create structures that enable organic community growth—all essential for developer community work.

Storytelling & Communication

Crafting engaging narratives in D&D has honed my ability to tell compelling stories about technology, products, and ideas. Whether presenting at conferences or writing technical content, storytelling makes concepts memorable and engaging.

Current Gaming Involvement

While my public play organizing has reduced as I've focused on professional pursuits, I remain actively involved in tabletop gaming:

  • Running private campaigns for friends and colleagues
  • Participating in online gaming through virtual tabletops
  • Staying current with new RPG systems and gaming trends
  • Mentoring new dungeon masters through online communities
  • Advocating for inclusive gaming practices and accessibility
